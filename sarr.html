Search and Rescue Robot

As part of MAE 322: Mechanical Design with Professor Daniel Nosenchuck, I worked in a group of 11 students on an 8-week project to design, manufacture, and automate a Search and Rescue Robot (SaRR).

The robot was required to survive a 1-foot drop test, track light, navigate ramps and chutes, breach a 1-foot wall, and deliver a medical payload to a randomized drop point.

As Electrical Lead, I designed and assembled the full wiring harnesses for both robots and served as the primary systems integrator during programming, testing, and debugging.
